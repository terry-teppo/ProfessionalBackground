📊 Methodology

This calculator uses data from "The Global Labor Economics of Open-Source Software" (Teppo, 2025).

The model applies a wage elasticity of 0.3 (standard for skilled labor) with role-specific substitution rates based on OSS tool dependency.

The U.S. outsources 6.9%–10.9% of global OSS labor to unpaid contributors abroad, suppressing domestic IT wages by 13%–62% depending on role.

Key findings:

  • U.S. does only 4% of global OSS work
  • U.S. has 12. 9% of global developers
  • Gap = 6.9%–10.9% outsourced labor
  • $450B–$1.9T in suppressed wages annually
